# New Shader Plan: Luminescent Quantum-Glass Phoenix-Egg

## Overview
A hyper-organic, slowly revolving cosmic egg forged from multi-layered, refractive quantum glass that encases a swirling, aggressively pulsating embryo of pure auroral aether-plasma, reacting intensely to acoustic life-frequencies.

## Features
- Volumetric, multi-layered refractive glass shell using stacked domain warping.
- Pulsating aether-plasma core (embryo) driven by fractional Brownian motion (fBm) and time.
- Audio-reactive 'heartbeat' mechanism that scales and distorts the core based on `u.config.y` (audio/click).
- Subsurface scattering simulation for the inner plasma illuminating the glass.
- Complex internal tendrils generated by 3D Voronoi noise mapped to spherical coordinates.
- Chromatic aberration and inner light refraction based on view angle and surface normals.
- Deep, cosmic void background with subtle, slowly drifting quantum dust particles.

## Technical Implementation
- File: public/shaders/gen-luminescent-quantum-glass-phoenix-egg.wgsl
- Category: generative
- Tags: ["organic", "quantum", "refractive", "plasma", "volumetric"]
- Algorithm: Raymarching an SDF sphere with multi-layered noise-displaced internal geometry, combined with volumetric accumulation for the plasma core and refractive index simulation for the outer shell.

### Core Algorithm
Raymarcher setup targeting a central sphere SDF. The sphere's surface is lightly displaced using high-frequency, low-amplitude Simplex noise to create a slightly organic, uneven glass texture. The interior is rendered by accumulating density along the ray once inside the sphere. The plasma core uses a smaller sphere SDF heavily displaced by animated 3D fBm noise (using `u.config.x` for time).

### Mouse Interaction
The mouse (`u.zoom_config.y`, `u.zoom_config.z`) controls the camera orbit, rotating the view around the egg. Additionally, the proximity of the mouse to the center pulls the internal plasma tendrils towards the camera via a localized gravity well distortion function applied to the inner domain.

### Color Mapping / Shading
The outer glass uses a physical-based approach with Schlick's approximation for Fresnel reflection, reflecting an ambient cosmic environment. The inner plasma core uses a fiery gradient mapping (from deep crimson to bright gold and electric blue, modified by UI sliders). A bloom effect and simulated subsurface scattering blend the core's intense light into the surrounding glass shell.
