# KIMI BRIEF — luma-melt-interactive

## Creative Spark
A living fluid surface where bright pixels melt downhill like hot wax, but the melt follows a real 2D curl-noise flow field so it spirals and pools instead of just falling straight. Mouse leaves persistent heat trails that slowly cool and fade. Depth makes foreground wax thick and slow; background wax runs fast and thin.

This week we are pushing: **liquid metal / viscous flow aesthetics + divergence-free advection + depth-aware viscosity**. Do not produce generic downward scrolling.

## Differentiate From
- `liquid-lens` (image): spherical glass refraction — yours is viscous flow, not lensing
- `neon-pulse-stream` (Batch B): fluid neon trails — yours is dense, slow wax melt with history
- `gen-superfluid-quantum-foam` (Kimi A): quantum bubbles — yours is macroscopic viscous flow

## Wow Mandate
The moment someone holds the mouse down and drags, they should see a persistent heat channel that continues to melt and flow for 3–4 seconds after they stop moving. Bass makes the whole surface turbulent; silence makes it glass-smooth.
