# KIMI BRIEF — pixel-stretch-interactive

## Creative Spark
A spatial slit-scan stretch from the mouse position to the screen edge, but each RGB channel stretches at a different intensity — red stretches farthest, blue least — creating chromatic smear along the stretch direction. Audio bass elongates the stretch dramatically. Depth makes background pixels stretch more than foreground, creating a depth-based temporal feel.

This week we are pushing: **chromatic directional stretch + audio elongation + depth-based stretch intensity**. Do not produce simple duplicate pixel lines or uniform RGB shifts.

## Differentiate From
- `temporal-rgb-smear` (Kimi A): time-based RGB trail — yours is spatial, not temporal
- `chroma-shift-grid` (image): per-block chromatic shift — yours is continuous directional smear
- `pixel-sand` (Kimi A): particle physics — yours is pure image manipulation

## Wow Mandate
During a bass drop, the stretch should elongate so far that the image becomes abstract streaks of pure color, then snap back to recognizability on the beat. The depth variation should make foreground subjects feel "solid" while backgrounds dissolve into streaks.

## Target
125 lines. Math density over commentary.
